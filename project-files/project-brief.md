# Jikanban (JKB) — Project Brief

## What Is This?

Jikanban is a personal project management tool that connects your task board to your project knowledge base. It lets you organize work across different disciplines — writing, engineering, customer communication, research — and uses AI to help you create tasks and stay aligned with your goals.

## The Problem

When you work across multiple types of work simultaneously, it's easy to get locked into one area and let everything else go stale. You might spend two weeks deep in technical work and not notice that you haven't written a blog post, followed up on a customer email, or done any research in that entire time.

Most task management tools don't help with this. They track what you're doing but have no awareness of what you *should* be doing or why.

## The Solution

A task board where each type of work has its own workflow, and an AI assistant that understands your project goals. When you create a task, the AI draws from your existing notes and documents to help you write it. Your task history is fed back into your knowledge base so you can ask questions like "what have I been spending most of my time on?" or "which areas have I been neglecting?"

## Who Is It For?

Solo builders, indie developers, and small teams who manage work across multiple disciplines and want a tool that understands their project — not just their to-do list.

## How It Works

1. You organize your work into boards — one per type of work (e.g. Development, Blog Posts, Customer Emails)
2. Each board has its own stages that cards move through, from Backlog to Complete
3. Cards can have due dates and optional milestone dates per stage so nothing slips without warning
4. When creating a card, an AI assistant drafts the task description using your project notes as context
5. Your board activity syncs back to your knowledge base so you can analyze patterns and get insights over time

## What Makes It Different

- It connects your task management to your knowledge base — the two inform each other
- It runs entirely on your own infrastructure — your data stays yours
- It uses local AI by default — no data leaves your network unless you choose otherwise
